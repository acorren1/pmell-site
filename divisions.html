Quick Age Guide

Where Players Start

Use this as a general guide. Final placement can depend on league eligibility, experience, tryouts, and roster needs.

Entry Level

Ages 4–6

Tee Ball

This program is for players who have never played ball before and is designed to provide a fun foundation and introduction to baseball and softball.

Focus Areas
  • Fun
  • Fitness
  • Fundamentals
Developmental

Ages 6–8

Coach Pitch

This program is for younger players with less experience and continues the introduction to baseball through fun, fitness, and fundamentals.

Eligibility Note

6-year-olds must have completed one season in Tee Ball to be eligible.

Skill Building

Ages 8–11

Minors Baseball / Softball

This program is for players with less experience and continues building a foundation in baseball and softball. Players begin learning to pitch, catch, and develop more advanced skills.

Important Notes

Players ages 9 and up may be drafted to Majors Baseball if they have tried out.

8-year-olds must have completed one season in Coach Pitch to be eligible.

Competitive

Ages 9–12

Majors Baseball / Softball

Though still instructional, the Major League division is more competitive than the other divisions. Players must try out and are drafted each year to a team.

Tryout & Draft Info

If a player is not drafted into Majors, they would play at the Minor level and may try out again next season.

Returning Majors PlayersIf a player was drafted onto a Major League division team the previous spring season, they remain on that team for the remainder of their eligibility and do not have to try out again.
Tournament TeamsTournament teams are selected from this level at the end of the season to participate in the District All Stars Tournament.
Transition Division

Ages 11–13

Intermediate 50/70 Baseball

This division offers a transition between the standard Little League field size and the Junior/Senior/Big League field size.

Field Specs

50-foot pitching distance

70-foot base paths

Advanced RulesMany Junior League rules are used, including lead-offs, stealing at any time, and allowing an on-deck batter.
Dual Roster Note11- and 12-year-old Majors Baseball players may dual roster on an Intermediate team for an additional fee. Majors schedules take precedence.
Tournament EligibilityPlayers are only eligible to be selected onto one tournament team at the end of the season.
Older Players

Ages 13–16

Seniors Baseball

This competitive program is designed to develop baseball players on MLB field dimensions and prepare players for higher levels of play.

Tournament teams are selected from this level at the end of the season to participate in the District All Stars Tournament.

Baseball Specs

60-foot, 6-inch pitching distance

90-foot base paths

Older Players

Ages 13–16

Seniors Softball

This competitive program is designed to develop softball players and prepare girls for PIAA or High School softball.

Tournament teams are selected from this level at the end of the season to participate in the District All Stars Tournament.

Softball Specs

12-inch ball

Pitch from 43 feet
